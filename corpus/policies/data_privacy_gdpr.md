# Data Privacy and GDPR Policy

*Effective: 2026-01-01 · Owner: Data Protection Office · ACME GmbH*

## Scope and Responsibilities

This policy defines how ACME GmbH handles personal data of customers, employees, and business partners in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG). It applies to every employee and contractor who processes personal data in any ACME system, including the order-management system, the support ticket system, and the HR portal. The Data Protection Officer (DPO) can be reached at dpo@acme.de and is the escalation point for all questions in this policy.

## Deletion Requests (Right to Erasure)

Customer requests to delete their personal data are honored within 30 days of receipt. Support agents who receive a deletion request — by email, phone, or ticket — must log it in the ticket system with the tag "gdpr-deletion" and forward it to the DPO on the same business day. The DPO coordinates deletion across all systems, including backups per the backup rotation schedule. Data that ACME is legally required to retain (for example, invoices under German commercial and tax law, retained for 10 years) is excluded from deletion but is blocked from any further processing. The customer receives written confirmation once deletion is complete, within the 30-day deadline.

## Subject-Access Requests (SARs)

Subject-access requests — where a person asks what personal data ACME holds about them — are answered within one month of receipt, as required by Art. 15 GDPR. Agents must verify the requester's identity before any data is disclosed, using the email address and order details on file; if identity cannot be verified, the DPO decides on additional verification steps. The response includes the categories of data processed, the purposes, recipients, retention periods, and a copy of the data itself. The one-month deadline may be extended by up to two further months only for complex cases, and only with DPO approval and written notice to the requester within the first month.

## PII Export Restrictions

Support agents may not export customer PII without DPO approval. This covers any bulk extraction of personal data from ACME systems — CSV downloads, copies into spreadsheets, forwarding of customer lists by email, or pasting customer records into external tools, including AI services not approved by IT Security. Viewing individual customer records inside the order-management or ticket system as part of normal case handling is permitted and does not require approval. Requests for exports (for example, for an analytics project) must be submitted to dpo@acme.de with the business purpose, data fields, and retention plan; the DPO responds within 5 business days.

## Data Minimization and Retention

ACME collects and stores only the personal data needed for a defined purpose. Employees must not collect extra data fields "just in case", must use pseudonymized or aggregated data whenever the task allows, and must not store customer data in personal drives, private notes, or chat messages. Support tickets are anonymized 24 months after closure; inactive customer accounts are deleted after 36 months unless legal retention duties apply. Any suspected personal-data breach must be reported immediately to security@acme.de and dpo@acme.de so ACME can meet the 72-hour supervisory-authority notification deadline under Art. 33 GDPR.
